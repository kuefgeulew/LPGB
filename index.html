🏦 1. Bancassurance
Definition

A partnership between a bank and an insurance company where the bank acts as a sales channel for insurance products to its existing customer base.

Example

You visit BRAC Bank to open an account. The officer also offers you a MetLife insurance policy. The bank earns a commission; MetLife gains a new customer — that's bancassurance.

✅

Prospects in Bangladesh

  • People trust banks → easier insurance sales
  • Expanding middle class = more customers
  • Promotes financial inclusion
  • Cost-effective distribution channel
⚠️

Challenges

  • Low public awareness of insurance
  • Bank staff need specialised training
  • Complex regulatory approvals
  • Conflict of interest risk
💱 2. Crawling Peg System
Definition

A system where a country's currency value is adjusted gradually and incrementally rather than in sudden large movements — giving businesses time to plan.

Example

1 USD = 120 BDT. Government moves it slowly: 121 → 122 → 123 BDT over months, instead of a sudden jump to 130 BDT. Exporters can plan; market panic is avoided.

💰 3. Deposit Mix
Definition

The variety/composition of a bank's deposits — savings, current, and fixed — used to balance short-term liquidity and long-term lending capacity.

Deposit TypeNatureInterestLiquidity
Fixed DepositsLong-termHighestLow
Savings AccountsMedium-termModerateMedium
Current AccountsShort-termNoneHighest
Analogy

Like keeping some money in a piggy bank (emergency/current) and some in DPS (long-term/fixed). The mix helps the bank stay liquid while also earning from loans.

💵 4. Treasury Bonds
Definition

A long-term loan you give to the Bangladesh Government. The government promises to repay with interest at fixed intervals.

Example

You buy a 10-year Bangladesh Treasury Bond worth Tk. 1 lakh. You lend money to the government; they pay you interest every 6 months and return your principal at maturity.

💶 5. Repo & Reverse Repo

📥 Repo (Bank borrows from BB)

  • Bank sells securities to BB
  • Promises to repurchase later
  • Bank gets short-term liquidity
  • BRAC Bank sells bonds to BB today, buys back tomorrow

📤 Reverse Repo (BB borrows from Banks)

  • BB buys securities from banks
  • Banks park excess funds with BB
  • Absorbs excess liquidity from market
  • BB buys bonds to inject / absorb money supply
⚙️ 6. Central Bank as Lender of Last Resort
Definition

When all commercial banks run short of funds, Bangladesh Bank provides emergency liquidity to prevent bank runs and systemic collapse.

Example

City Bank faces a sudden rush of withdrawals and runs short of cash. It borrows from Bangladesh Bank to meet demand — preventing public panic.

✍️ 7. Crossing a Cheque
Definition

Drawing two parallel lines on the face of a cheque means it cannot be cashed over the counter — it must be deposited into a bank account. This adds security.

Example

Your employer gives you a crossed salary cheque. You cannot withdraw cash at the counter — you must deposit it into your account. This creates a paper trail and prevents theft.

📌 Important Note on "Bearer → Order" Alteration

Even if a cheque is changed from "bearer" to "order," this is NOT a material alteration — it only adds safety. The cheque remains legally valid and the drawer must still pay.

🪙 8. Demand Draft (DD)
Definition

A pre-paid payment instrument issued by a bank guaranteeing payment to a specific named payee. Cannot be made payable to Bearer — must always name a specific payee.

Example — DD Payable to Bearer NOT Allowed

You cannot write "Pay Bearer" on a DD. It must say "Pay Mr. Rahim or Order." This prevents fraud and theft.

⚖️ 9. Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)
Definition

Settling legal/financial disputes without going to court — faster, cheaper, and less adversarial.

🗣️

Negotiation

You and the bank talk directly to resolve the issue — no third party involved.

🤝

Mediation

A neutral third party helps both sides communicate and reach an agreement.

⚖️

💡 14. Financial Literacy
Definition

Knowing how to earn, save, spend, invest, and protect money wisely — making informed financial decisions in daily life.

Example

A person who budgets monthly income, pays bills on time, saves for emergencies, avoids scams, and invests for the future is financially literate. Banks run programs to teach these skills to the public.

🚗 15. Lease Finance
Definition

Using an asset without owning it — the lessee pays regular rent to the lessor (NBFI/bank) for use of the asset, with an option to buy at the end.

Example

A company leases a photocopier from IDLC for 3 years. They use it, pay monthly rent, and can return or buy it at the end. This preserves working capital — they don't need to buy outright.

🧾 16. Bangladesh Bank's Monitoring of Classified Loans
Classified Loans

Loans where borrowers don't repay on time. Bangladesh Bank tracks these using CIB reports (Credit Information Bureau) and loan rescheduling rules.

⚠️ Key Problem

Many banks misuse rescheduling facilities — they keep rescheduling loans every year instead of actually recovering them, hiding the true default rate.

Example

A big borrower keeps rescheduling his loan every year instead of repaying. The bank avoids showing it as a default. Bangladesh Bank's steps are good, but stronger monitoring, skilled staff, and punishment for misuse are still needed.

🏦 18. Drawing Power & Power of Attorney

💳 Drawing Power (DP)

  • Maximum amount a borrower can withdraw from a loan account
  • Based on the value of goods pledged to the bank
  • Bank typically lends 60–80% of pledged goods' value
  • Goods worth Tk.50 lakh pledged, bank allows 70% → DP = Tk.35 lakh

📜 Power of Attorney (POA)

  • Written authority given to someone to act on your behalf
  • Can cover banking, property, legal, business transactions
  • Must be registered for property matters
  • Businessman in Dubai gives his brother POA to handle his BRAC Bank account

🧩 23. General Banker–Customer Relationship

📌 Core Principle

When a customer opens an account, both sides enter a legal contract under the Contract Act 1872. The bank verifies identity through KYC before accepting. The relationship changes direction depending on who owes whom:

  • You deposit money → you are the creditor, bank is the debtor
  • You take a loan → you are the debtor, bank is the creditor

⚖️ 25. Special Relationship — Bank's Obligations
✅

Honour Cheques

Bank must pay valid cheques if: properly drawn, sufficient balance, correct signature, presented within reasonable time. If Tk.5,000 cheque presented with Tk.10,000 balance → bank must honour it.

🔒

Maintain Secrecy

Bank must keep account information private — even after account closure — except by court order or legal requirement. Bank cannot reveal your loan balance to outsiders.

💸

Right to Charge Interest/Fees

Banks can charge service fees — processing, appraisal, penal interest, SMS alerts, etc. Published in their Schedule of Charges.

🔐

Banker's Lien

Bank can retain goods or securities until dues are cleared, and sell them after notice if unpaid. Two types: General Lien (any property for any dues) and Particular Lien (specific items for that specific loan).

🏦 26. Rights of a Banker
🔒

Right of Lien

Legal power to hold property or securities until debt is paid.

  • General Lien: Any property held by bank for any dues owed
  • Particular Lien: Only specific items linked to that specific loan
Example

Bank keeps your FDR as lien until loan repayment is complete.

➕

Right of Set-Off

Allows bank to adjust a customer's credit balance in one account against debit in another.

Example

You owe Tk.1 lakh on loan but have Tk.40,000 in savings — bank can set off Tk.40,000 automatically from your savings to reduce the loan balance.

🚪 27. Termination of Banker–Customer Relationship

🏦 By Banker

💵 55. Payment in Due Course
Definition

Payment made by a bank according to the instrument's terms, in good faith, without negligence, and to the right person. Even if later found invalid, the bank is not liable if it paid in due course.

🇧🇩 56. Inland Instrument
Definition

A promissory note, bill of exchange, or cheque that is drawn and payable within the same country.

Example

Cheque drawn at BRAC Bank Dhaka, payable at EBL Chittagong — entirely within Bangladesh = inland instrument.

✍️ 57. Endorsement
Definition

Signing the back of a cheque or bill to transfer ownership/title to another person. The endorser signs; the endorsee receives it.

Example

A gives cheque to B. B signs the back and gives to C. C is now the new holder with full rights to collect payment. This chain: A → B → C creates a transfer of title.

⚠️ 58. Material Alteration
Definition

An unauthorised change to key terms of a negotiable instrument (amount, date, payee, etc.). It invalidates the document unless all parties consent.

Example

Changing Tk.500 to Tk.1,000 without the drawer's consent = material alteration → cheque becomes void.

📌 Note: Bearer → Order is NOT Material Alteration

Changing "bearer" to "order" only adds safety. It does NOT invalidate the cheque. The drawer must still pay.

🏦 59. Collecting Bank vs Paying Banker

🏦 Collecting Bank

  • Bank that collects cheque amount for its customer from the paying bank
  • Receives cheque, verifies, sends for clearing
  • Credits customer account on realisation
  • Mr. B deposits cheque at Bank X → Bank X collects from Bank Y

💳 Paying Banker

  • Bank on which the cheque is drawn — makes the payment
  • Verifies signature, balance, stop payment
  • Debits drawer's account
  • Mr. A writes cheque on Bank Y → Bank Y is the paying banker

❌ 62. When Can Bank Refuse a Cheque?
  • Insufficient funds in account
  • Account frozen or stopped
  • Signature does not match specimen
  • Stop payment instruction received
  • Stale cheque (older than 6 months)
  • Post-dated (future date not yet reached)
  • Material alteration detected
  • Drawer deceased/mentally incapacitated
✅ 63. Positive Pay Instruction
Definition

A fraud prevention system where the customer submits cheque details (number, amount, date, payee) to the bank in advance. Bank cross-verifies before clearing. Any mismatch triggers a hold.

💸 64. Hundi
Definition

A traditional informal money-transfer method in South Asia. NOT a legally enforceable negotiable instrument.

✅ Positive Impact

  • Helps migrant workers send money easily
  • Fast and accessible in remote areas
  • Lower transaction costs

❌ Negative Impact

  • Promotes money laundering
  • Enables tax evasion
  • Loss of government foreign exchange revenue
⚖️ 65. Garnishee Order
Definition

A court order allowing a creditor to collect a debtor's money directly from a third party (like a bank or employer) without the debtor's involvement.

Example

A court orders a bank to deduct loan repayments directly from a customer's salary account each month — the customer cannot stop this deduction.

😴 72. Dormant Accounts

⏳ When Does an Account Become Dormant?

  • Current/SND accounts → dormant after 6 months of no transactions
  • Savings accounts → dormant after 18 months of no transactions
  • Dormant for 5 years → full new KYC required to reactivate
  • Unclaimed for 10 years → balance transferred to Bangladesh Bank

💤 73. Unclaimed Deposit Accounts
Rule

If a customer cannot be traced after long inactivity, the bank transfers the balance to Bangladesh Bank as Unclaimed Deposit Account. All unclaimed deposits/valuables after 10 years go to Bangladesh Bank (under Banking Company Act Section 35).

❌ 74. Closing an Account
Procedure

Done by written notice or account closure form. Bank returns remaining balance + any accrued interest, deducting applicable charges. Customer must return cheque books, debit cards, and any other instruments.

🔐 90. Vault Cash Handling Procedure
📋

Record Details

Record denomination details of all notes and coins on vouchers before moving any cash.

👥

Dual Control — Manager + Cash Officer

Cash is always moved in or out by Manager and Cash Officer together — never one person alone.

🔑

Dual Keys — Both Required

Both must use their own separate keys to open the safe. Never allow one person to operate both keys.

🚫

Authorised Key Holders Only

Only authorised key holders can handle safe keys. Keys must never be shared, duplicated, or left unattended.

🔐 91. Safe Deposit Locker & Safe Custody
🛡️

Safe Deposit Locker

Rented secure space with dual keys (bank key + customer key). Both keys needed to open. Strict entry/exit control. Nothing illegal allowed.

📜

Safe Custody

Bank keeps important documents (Wills, Title Deeds, Contracts) for a fee. Sealed and stored — bank doesn't read contents.

🏆

Benefits

  • Protection from theft, fire, flood
  • Confidentiality — key holders only
  • Long-term storage for heirlooms, deeds
  • Peace of mind and insurance

The GL records all assets, liabilities, equity, income, and expenses. The accounting rule: Debits = Credits for balance. Reconciliation = comparing GL with statements to ensure accuracy and catch errors.
